Bhagat Singh

1907 – 1931
“They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit.”

Biography

Bhagat Singh was one of the most influential revolutionaries of the Indian independence movement.

He became a symbol of youth's courage, sacrifice, and resistance against British colonial rule.

Bhagat Singh actively participated in acts of protest, including the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929, to awaken the nation against injustice.

He was executed at the young age of 23, but his ideals and bravery continue to inspire generations of Indians.
