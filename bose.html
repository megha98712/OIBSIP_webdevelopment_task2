Subhas Chandra Bose

1897 – 1945
“Give me blood, and I will give you freedom.”

Biography

Subhas Chandra Bose, popularly known as Netaji, was a prominent freedom fighter who led the Indian National Army (INA) against British colonial rule. His vision was complete independence for India.

Bose advocated for armed struggle to overthrow British rule, believing it was necessary alongside nonviolent methods for achieving freedom.

He formed the Indian National Army (INA) and inspired thousands of Indians to fight for the nation with courage and determination.

Subhas Chandra Bose passed away in 1945 under mysterious circumstances, but his legacy of bravery and patriotism continues to inspire generations.
