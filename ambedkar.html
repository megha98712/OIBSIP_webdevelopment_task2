Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

1891 – 1956
“Cultivation of mind should be the ultimate aim of human existence.”

Biography

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, popularly known as Baba Saheb Ambedkar, was the chief architect of the Indian Constitution.

He was a social reformer, economist, and politician who fought against social discrimination toward untouchables (Dalits) in India.

Ambedkar emphasized the importance of education, equality, and social justice, and worked tirelessly to uplift marginalized communities.

He passed away on 6 December 1956, but his vision of equality, liberty, and fraternity continues to inspire generations.
